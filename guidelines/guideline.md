# Diabetes Guidelines

## 1. Diagnostic Criteria for T2DM and Prediabetes
Diabetes is diagnosed using Fasting Plasma Glucose (FPG), 2-hour Plasma Glucose (2-h PG) during a 75g OGTT, or HbA1c[cite: 2]. Unless symptomatic, diagnoses must be confirmed by repeat testing[cite: 2].

### Table 01: Diagnostic Criteria
| Test | Normal | Prediabetes (IFG/IGT) | Diabetes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **FBG** | < 100 mg/dl | 100 to 125 mg/dl | ≥ 126 mg/dl[cite: 2] |
| **RBG / 75g OGTT** | < 140 mg/dl | 140 to 199 mg/dl | ≥ 200 mg/dl[cite: 2] |
| **HbA1c** | < 5.7% | 5.7 to 6.4% | ≥ 6.5%[cite: 2] |

*   **Symptomatic Presentation:** A random plasma glucose $\ge200\text{ mg/dl}$ with classic symptoms is sufficient for diagnosis[cite: 2].

## 2. Glycemic Targets
Glycemic goals should be tailored to individual patient profiles, risk of hypoglycemia, and comorbidities[cite: 2].

### Table 02: Glycemic Targets
| Sub Category | Fasting (FBG) | Random (RBG) | Bed Time | HbA1c (%) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Recent / no complications** | 80–120 mg/dl | 80–160 mg/dl | 100–140 mg/dl | < 7.0%[cite: 2] |
| **With CCF, CKD, CLD** | 100–140 mg/dl | 120–180 mg/dl | 120–180 mg/dl | 7.0–8.0%[cite: 2] |

*   **Strict Target:** $\le6.5\%$ for selected patients with short duration and long life expectancy[cite: 2].
*   **Relaxed Target:** Up to $8.5\%$ for frail or elderly patients[cite: 2].

## 3. Risk Assessment & Prevalence
*   **Prevalence:** Pakistan ranks 3rd globally in diabetes prevalence (33 million affected)[cite: 2].
*   **Screening:** Use the locally validated **RAPID** scoring system[cite: 2]. High-risk individuals require lab testing, and those with normal results should receive counseling and repeat testing annually[cite: 2].

## 4. Non-Pharmacological Management
*   **DSME:** Focus on patient empowerment, SMBG, foot care, and hypoglycemia awareness[cite: 2].
*   **Nutrition:** Limit simple sugars and carbohydrates; emphasize fiber, lean protein, and vegetables[cite: 2]. Avoid trans fats entirely[cite: 2]. Energy requirement: $25-30\text{ kcal/kg}$ of adjusted body weight[cite: 2].
*   **Activity:** Walk $\ge30$ minutes/day, 5 days/week[cite: 2]. Carry a glucose source if on insulin/secretagogues[cite: 2].
*   **Remission:** Weight loss of $10-15\text{ kg}$ via $800\text{ kcal}$ diet for $12-20$ weeks can induce remission in patients diagnosed within 6 years[cite: 2].

## 5. Pharmacological Management
*   **First-Line:** Metformin + lifestyle modifications[cite: 2]. Contraindicated if $\text{eGFR}<30\text{ ml/min/1.73 m}^2$[cite: 2].
*   **Combination:** Recommended for HbA1c $>8.5\%$ at diagnosis[cite: 2].
*   **Agents:** 
    *   **SGLT2i:** Benefit in CKD; contraindicated if $\text{eGFR}<30$[cite: 2].
    *   **GLP-1 RA:** Preferred to insulin; promotes weight loss[cite: 2].
    *   **DPP-4i:** Weight-neutral, low hypoglycemia risk[cite: 2].
    *   **Sulfonylureas:** Effective ($1.5\%$ HbA1c reduction) but watch for hypoglycemia/weight gain[cite: 2].
*   **Insulin:** Start $0.1-0.2\text{ U/kg}$ if patient is catabolic, symptomatic, or HbA1c $\ge10\%$[cite: 2].

## 6. Microvascular Complications
*   **Retinopathy:** Annual/bi-annual dilated eye exams[cite: 2].
*   **Nephropathy:** Screen annually for microalbuminuria; track eGFR[cite: 2]. Use ACEi/ARBs and SGLT2i for renal protection[cite: 2].
*   **Neuropathy:** 10g monofilament test at presentation[cite: 2]. Screen for autonomic symptoms (gastroparesis, ED)[cite: 2].

## 7. Diabetic Foot Care
*   Inspect feet at every clinic visit[cite: 2].
*   Patients with ulcers or "feet at risk" require immediate referral to a multidisciplinary diabetic foot clinic[cite: 2].
